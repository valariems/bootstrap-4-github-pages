Explore organizations

Finding your fit at university.

Background

One of the first projects I tackled when I joined OrgSync in 2012 was a redesign of the organization listings at a university. The existing utilitarian page looked more like a grueling spreadsheet than an inviting showcase of campus clubs.

The legacy layout

Project goals

Design refresh.
Appeal to our audience of college students accustomed to seeing better information presentation on other sites they frequented.

Better information.
Give students more information about an organization that might entice them to become involved on campus.

Improved filtering.
Provide tools that give students flexibility to find exactly what theyâ€™re looking for in a way that encourages exploration.

Wireframe exploration

We had feedback telling us what other sites were used by our audience, and what our users expected to see on ours. After additional research, I made the decision to explore featuring organization profile photos. Popups on hover would allow additional information to be presented about an organization from its card on the page. This would allow users to get information like the organization's next event, without cluttering or distracting from the primary page information.

A small sample of wireframe concepts created

The final result

The final design, after several round of design iterations, was handed off to the development team to implement the advanced search and filtering functionality, as well as the infinite scrolll, optimized for efficient page load times.
